"Knowing one thing that you can clearly explain to others is better than knowing a thousand things that you can't explain." -- Jihoo Lee, June 17th, 2026 For example, let's say you know how to solve questions like 3(x-7):5=2(x+2):3(비례식을 사용한 일차방정식 question that i made up). If you cant explain it, does that really mean you know how to? Isn't that weird? Not knowing to explain but just to solve? Is saying "Because my teacher told so" is a valid excuse on not knowing to explain? That's the source, not the reason! The source isn't the reason why! Just don't go too far making kids explain 2+2=4 without explaining the axioms of arithmetic. That would be university level, not kindergarden level. 'I know how to solve real difficult questions but I can't explain an easy question.' That would be knowing the procedure only, not actually knowing the reason why and referencing your teacher. "My teacher said so" Oh so on a job interview when you get a question about a reason, you are gonna say that your teacher said so?
